Jagadish Chandra Bose

Pioneer in Radio Waves and Plant Science

Introduction

Jagadish Chandra Bose was an Indian physicist, biologist, botanist, and a pioneer in the investigation of radio and microwave optics. He was also the first to demonstrate that plants respond to external stimuli — a revolutionary idea at the time. His life and work remain a source of inspiration for generations of scientists and inventors.

Early Life

He was born on November 30, 1858, in Mymensingh, Bengal Presidency (present-day Bangladesh). He studied in Calcutta and later went to the University of Cambridge to study natural sciences. After returning to India, he became a professor at Presidency College, Calcutta.

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1858: Born in Mymensingh, Bengal.
  • 1884: Traveled to England to study at Cambridge University.
  • 1885: Returned to India and joined Presidency College as a professor.
  • 1894–1899: Researched radio waves and proved plants can feel pain and respond to stimuli.
  • 1917: Founded the Bose Institute in Kolkata.
  • 1937: Passed away on November 23 in Giridih, Bihar.

Major Contributions

  • Invented the Crescograph — a device to measure plant growth and response to stimuli.
  • One of the earliest scientists to prove radio waves could travel through different materials.
  • Conducted path-breaking experiments in plant physiology.
  • Published research papers and even wrote science fiction in Bengali.

Famous Quote

"The true laboratory is the mind, where behind illusions we uncover the laws of truth."
— Jagadish Chandra Bose

Legacy

Bose's interdisciplinary approach to science was ahead of his time. He is considered the father of modern science in India, and many institutions and awards are named in his honor. His dedication to both scientific discovery and education remains legendary.
